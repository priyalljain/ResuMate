# Job Description: Growth Marketing Manager — Founding Team

**Company:** Redrob AI (Series A AI-native talent intelligence platform)
**Location:** Pune/Noida, India (Hybrid) | Open to relocation candidates from Tier-1 Indian cities
**Employment Type:** Full-time
**Experience Required:** 4–7 years (soft range, not a hard cutoff)

## Let's be honest about this role

We're a Series A company building our core marketing engine from scratch. We don't need a high-level strategist who wants to sit in meetings and manage agency vendors all day. We need a scrappy, data-obsessed growth marketer who is simultaneously comfortable diving deep into analytics dashboards (attribution, CAC/LTV, funnels) AND opening Figma or writing copy to launch a campaign themselves. We'd rather you tilt slightly toward a relentless experimenter and executioner than a traditional brand marketer.

## What you'd actually be doing

Own the inbound acquisition funnel for Redrob’s B2B recruitment platform. Weeks 1-3: audit our current messy setup across Google Ads, LinkedIn, and basic content loops. Weeks 4-8: launch a v2 performance marketing strategy, fix our broken attribution model, and ship 3 major content/lead-gen experiments. Weeks 9-12: build out a scalable marketing automation system (HubSpot/Marketo) and establish clear pipeline reporting metrics for the leadership team. Beyond that: scale our customer acquisition cost (CAC) efficiently and build the foundational marketing playbook as the company grows.

## Disqualifiers (explicit)

* Pure "brand awareness" or PR backgrounds with **no direct response, performance, or pipeline attribution** experience — will not move forward.
* Growth experience consisting primarily of inflating vanity metrics (likes, impressions, follower growth) **without** tying those metrics directly to revenue, SQLs, or product sign-ups — will probably not move forward.
* Mid-to-senior marketers who haven't built a campaign, written ad copy, or set up a tracking pixel in the last 18 months because they became pure "people managers" — will probably not move forward. This role executes.

## Things you absolutely need

* Proven track record of managing **paid acquisition channels** (Google Search, LinkedIn Ads, Meta) at a B2B SaaS or product company, scaling budgets while maintaining target CAC.
* Hands-on mastery of the **modern marketing tech stack** — marketing automation (HubSpot, Customer.io), web analytics (GA4, Mixpanel, Amplitude), and CRM tracking (Salesforce, HubSpot CRM).
* Deep understanding of **data analytics and attribution modeling** — you must be able to confidently navigate spreadsheets to prove exactly where our leads are coming from.
* Strong **copywriting skills** with a knack for translating complex tech/AI concepts into simple, high-converting copy.

## Things we'd like but won't reject you for

Experience in the HR-tech, talent acquisition, or marketplace space; technical SEO knowledge (programmatic SEO is a huge plus); basic design/video editing skills (Figma, Adobe Suite); experience marketing to developer or enterprise recruiter audiences.

## Things we explicitly do NOT want

* **Agency-only veterans**: People who have only worked at digital marketing agencies managing clients but have never owned the long-term, end-to-end growth trajectory of a single product.
* **Tool collectors**: People whose resume is just a list of marketing tools they've logged into, rather than a narrative of growth loops they've built. We care about systems and psychology, not just platforms.
* **Budget burning enthusiasts**: Marketers who only know how to scale acquisition by throwing massive amounts of VC money at paid ads without optimizing organic or product-led growth loops.
* **People who have only worked at massive conglomerates** (e.g., legacy IT services or slow-moving enterprises) where launching an ad takes 6 weeks of corporate approvals.

## Location, comp, logistics

Pune/Noida preferred but flexible; candidates in Hyderabad, Pune, Mumbai, Delhi NCR welcome. Outside India: case-by-case, no visa sponsorship. Notice period: sub-30-day strongly preferred; 30+ day candidates still in scope but the selection bar will be higher.

## The vibe check

Culture-fit matters more than skills-fit at this stage. We operate in an async-first environment, write heavily, disagree openly, and move incredibly fast. You must have a high tolerance for ambiguity, an immature data pipeline, and evolving product positioning.

## How to read between the lines (the ideal candidate)

Roughly: 5 years of total marketing experience, with at least 3 years spent embedded in a fast-growing **B2B SaaS product** company. They have successfully taken a product or feature from zero to meaningful user adoption. They have a strong, defensible philosophy on product-led growth vs. sales-led growth, attribution setups, and content scaling. They don't wait for a creative agency to give them an asset; if an ad needs to go live today, they build a scrappy version and ship it.

We are looking for a needle in a haystack—a marketer who thinks like an engineer and executes like a founder.

## Note for hackathon participants

The "right answer" is **not** to look for candidates who just list "SEO, SEM, PPC" as keywords on their resume—that is a superficial signal. The right answer involves looking for ownership and impact. A candidate who calls themselves a "Product Marketing Manager" but has explicitly built acquisition funnels and owned a revenue quota is a strong fit. A candidate with "Growth Marketing Director" on their title who has spent the last 4 years managing a 20-person team and a legacy brand budget is not a fit for this founding role.
